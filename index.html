About Mandhana

A Stellar Rise in Cricket

Smriti Mandhana's cricket journey began at an early age, nurtured by her cricket-loving family. She made her international debut at the tender age of 16, setting a new record as the youngest Indian woman to play in a One Day International (ODI). From that moment on, there was no looking back for the young prodigy.

Smriti Shriniwas Mandhana born 18 July 1996 is an Indian cricketer who represents the Indian women's national team. She plays for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the Women's Premier League (WPL). In domestic cricket, she represents the Maharashtra cricket team.

In June 2018, the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) awarded her 'the Best Women's International Cricketer' in BCCI awards.In December 2018, the International Cricket Council (ICC) awarded her with the Rachael Heyhoe-Flint Award for the best female cricketer of the year. On 30 December 2021, she became a nominee of the ICC Women's T20 Player of the Year. In December 2021, she, Tammy Beaumont, Lizelle Lee and Gaby Lewis were nominated for the ICC Women's Cricketer of the Year. In January 2022, the ICC awarded her with the Rachael Heyhoe-Flint Award for the ICC Women's Cricketer of the Year

Breaking Records and Creating history

Smriti's impeccable batting technique, combined with her aggressive style of play, quickly made her a force to be reckoned with. In 2018, she etched her name in history books by becoming the first Indian woman cricketer to score a century in a Women's Cricket Super League match.

⇒ Fastest Fifty by an Indian Woman in T20Is.
⇒ Youngest Indian Woman to Score a Century in ODIs.
⇒ Highest Opening Partnership in Women's ODIs (with Punam Raut).
⇒ Most Sixes in a Calendar Year in Women's ODIs.
⇒ Wisden's Leading Women Cricketer in the World (2019).

Her meteoric rise continued as she made waves in the international cricket circuit, becoming one of the most prolific run-scorers for India. Smriti's name adorned numerous record books, including being the fastest Indian woman to reach 1,000 runs in ODIs.
